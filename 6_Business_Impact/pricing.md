# Pricing Strategy

## Objective

Define a pricing model that aligns with customer value, supports scalable adoption, and reflects the impact of improved decision-making.

---

# 1. Pricing Model Overview

The product follows a **SaaS + usage-based hybrid model**.

| Component        | Pricing Basis                  | Rationale                          |
|------------------|--------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Base Platform     | Per operator (seat-based)      | Aligns with primary user value     |
| Usage Component   | Per call volume               | Scales with system utilization     |
| Advanced Features | Tier-based                    | Monetize additional capabilities   |

---

---

# 3. Value Alignment

| Pricing Dimension   | Customer Value Alignment                |
|--------------------|------------------------------------------|
| Per operator        | Direct user productivity                |
| Per call volume     | System usage and operational scale      |
| Tiered features     | Increasing organizational value         |

---

# 4. Buyer Justification

| Value Area            | Pricing Justification                  |
|------------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Time savings           | Faster decisions per call              |
| Accuracy improvement   | Reduced costly errors                  |
| Risk reduction         | Lower legal and compliance exposure    |
| Operational scale      | Standardized performance across teams  |

---

# 5. Adoption Strategy

| Stage              | Approach                              |
|--------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Initial rollout     | Pilot pricing / discounted entry       |
| Expansion           | Scale with operator count              |
| Enterprise rollout  | Contract-based pricing                 |

---

# 6. Pricing Trade-offs

| Trade-off                  | Decision                          | Rationale                          |
|----------------------------|----------------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Simplicity vs Precision    | Favor simplicity                 | Easier adoption                    |
| Fixed vs Usage-based       | Hybrid model                     | Align cost with value              |
| Low entry vs high margin   | Lower entry price                | Encourage adoption                 |

---

# Product Implication

The pricing model is designed to:

- Align cost with measurable value delivered  
- Enable gradual adoption and scaling  
- Support different customer segments  

Pricing evolves as the product demonstrates increased value through improved accuracy, efficiency, and insights.
